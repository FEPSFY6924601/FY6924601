Abstract

Time is important to all modern civilisations, and this article aims to examine its most prominent developments. Firstly, I explain how shadows were used to measure the passage of time and its limitations, then how mechanical clocks and marine chronometers improved accuracy by controlling motion. Finally, I explore how atomic clocks and clock-fusion systems generate precise results forming todays global time infrastructure.
