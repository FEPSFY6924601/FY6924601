Clock fusion

Modern “clock fusion” refers to the integration of multiple time sources such as a Caesium atomic clock, GNSS-discipline oscillators, and network-based feeds. They use hardware and algorithmic frameworks to enhance accuracy performance, creating a resilience against signal loss or manipulation. Rather than relying on a single best source, Fusion clocks operate by continuously comparing different inputs and weighing their reliability, switching their source to the most accurate, therefore outputting a stable time throughout. Furthermore, clock fusion mitigates various vulnerabilities by incorporating secure local oscillators, checking sources, and confirming signal integrity before distribution. This approach ensures continuity of precise time even when external reference degrades or fails, marking a strategic shift from unreliable single source clocks to distributed, verified resilient timekeeping.
